Root Cause Analysis: Rapido Rider Cancellations

Completed: December 2023
Root Cause Analysis, Marketplace Optimization

Background

Rapido has witnessed a significant increase in ride cancellations by riders, creating a negative trend that impacts multiple aspects of the business:

  • Erosion of rider trust in the platform
  • Reduced earnings for captains (drivers)
  • Operational inefficiencies and increased costs
  • Higher workload for customer support teams
  • Negative impact on marketplace liquidity and reliability

Through preliminary analysis, we identified that these cancellations aren't random occurrences but cluster around specific situations in the pickup and ride-allocation process.

Problem Statement

Rider-initiated cancellations have increased by approximately X%, negatively impacting marketplace reliability and overall customer experience. Rapido needs to identify the root causes behind these cancellations and implement both short-term fixes and long-term strategic solutions.

Top Reasons for Cancellation Spike

1. Captain Delay / Forced Rider-Cancellation Problem

This emerged as the primary cause of rider frustration. Riders frequently report situations where captains:

  • Accept rides but remain stationary or move away from the pickup point
  • Take excessively long routes to reach the pickup location
  • Call riders and explicitly ask them to cancel the ride

This behavior forces riders to cancel requests preemptively to avoid disappointment, creating a negative experience and eroding trust in the platform.

"I was asked by the captain to cancel the ride, and he also insisted on paying cash instead of using the app payment."

2. High Pickup Distance + Inaccurate ETA Forecasting

Many cancellations occur when the system assigns captains who are:

  • Located too far from the pickup point (2-4 km away)
  • Stuck in traffic with no clear path to the rider
  • Approaching from directions that significantly increase travel time

The ETA shown in the app often proves inaccurate, leading to rider frustration and subsequent cancellations.

"The ETA was supposed to be 4 minutes, but it actually took the captain 18 minutes to reach me. I had to cancel because I was getting late."

3. Incentive/Commission Misalignment & Cash-Ride Preference

Captains sometimes engage in behaviors that lead to forced cancellations:

  • Preferring nearby, cash-paying, or surge-priced rides
  • Accepting Rapido rides to maintain acceptance rates while looking for better options
  • Encouraging riders to cancel app bookings and pay directly to avoid platform commissions
"The captain asked me to pay him directly in cash and cancel the trip on the app to save on commission."

Root Cause Analysis Summary

Issue Category Specific Loophole Impact
Captain Operational Behavior Captain delays, asks rider to cancel Rider distrust, unfair cancellation charges
Matching Algorithm High pickup distance, inaccurate ETA Long wait times leading to rider cancellations
Incentive Structure Captains prefer cash/off-app rides Forced rider cancellations
System UX Gaps No real-time fee waiver for captain fault Rider bears cost and frustration
Enforcement Weakness No strict penalties for repeated offenders Problematic behavior continues

Proposed Solutions

Short-Term (0-4 weeks)

  • Auto "Cancel Without Fee": Allow instant, fee-free cancellation if the captain isn't moving toward the pickup or ETA exceeds threshold
  • Better Cancellation Reason Mapping: Add dedicated "Captain asked me to cancel" option with auto-refund and captain flagging
  • Pickup Distance Guardrails: Require rider confirmation for captain assignments beyond 1.5-2 km

Mid-Term (1-2 months)

  • Strengthen Captain Incentive Alignment: Reward punctual pickups, penalize forced cancellations, adjust commission for short-distance pickups
  • Improve ETA Prediction Engine: Incorporate real-time traffic, road closures, and captain movement patterns

Long-Term (2-4 months)

  • Automated Fraud Detection System: Identify patterns like stationary captains after acceptance, immediate post-acceptance calls to riders, excessive "rider canceled" patterns linked to specific captains
  • Implement appropriate penalties and temporary bans for identified offenders

Success Metrics

KPI Description
Rider Cancellation Rate Primary metric tracking overall cancellation frequency
Average Pickup ETA Time between ride assignment and pickup
% of Rides Auto-Waived for Captain Fault Measure of system identifying and addressing captain issues
Captain Reliability Score Frequency of forced cancellations per captain
NPS for Pickup Experience Rider satisfaction with pickup process
Support Tickets on Cancellations Volume of cancellation-related support requests

Final Summary

The ~X% increase in rider cancellations on Rapido stems from three primary factors: captain delays and forced cancellations, high pickup distances with inaccurate ETAs, and incentive misalignment encouraging off-app rides.

Addressing this challenge requires a combination of product changes, policy enforcement, and improved matching logic to rebuild rider trust and enhance platform reliability. The proposed solutions create a balanced approach that benefits both riders and captains while strengthening Rapido's marketplace dynamics.

Metrics Framework: WhatsApp Schedule Call Feature

Completed: January 2024
Product Metrics, KPIs, Feature Measurement

Problem Statement

"How can we effectively track the success, adoption, engagement, and reliability of the Schedule Call feature on WhatsApp?"

This case study outlines a comprehensive metrics framework to measure the performance of WhatsApp's Schedule Call feature, enabling data-driven decision making and continuous improvement.

Primary Metric (North Star)

Scheduled-Call Completion Rate (CCR)

Definition: The percentage of scheduled calls that actually result in successful connections.

Formula: CCR = (Number of scheduled calls successfully connected) / (Total scheduled calls created)

This serves as the primary indicator of feature value, measuring how effectively the feature solves the problem of missed calls.

Core Feature Metrics

Schedule Call Creation Rate

Number of scheduled calls created per 1000 WhatsApp users per day. This metric indicates feature awareness and adoption among the user base.

Schedule Call Reminder Open Rate

The percentage of users who open notifications or in-app reminders for scheduled calls. A low open rate indicates issues with reminder visibility or relevance.

Missed Scheduled Call Rate

Missed Call Rate = Scheduled Calls NOT started / Total Scheduled Calls. This metric directly measures the problem the feature aims to solve.

Notification Interaction Rate

Tracking user interactions with scheduled call notifications:

  • Notification opened
  • Notification dismissed
  • Notification ignored

This helps identify notification fatigue and optimize reminder timing and content.

Post-Improvement Metrics

After implementing the "Ringing at Scheduled Time" feature enhancement, we would track:

Auto-Ring Trigger Success Rate

How frequently the system successfully initiates scheduled ringing events. This measures the technical reliability of the feature.

Auto-Ring → Call Conversion Rate

Of users who received the scheduled ring, what percentage actually answered the call? This indicates whether the ringing feature effectively addresses missed calls.

Receiver Acceptance Rate

For calls between two users, the percentage where the receiver accepts the call after receiving the ring. A low rate suggests need for UX improvements or better consent flows.

Engagement & Retention Metrics

Repeat Usage Rate

The percentage of users who schedule additional calls after their first use. This is a strong indicator of perceived value.

Feature Stickiness

DAU/MAU ratio for users performing scheduled calls. This measures habit formation and regular usage patterns.

Multi-Party Scheduling Adoption

If group calling functionality is added, track adoption of scheduling calls with multiple participants.

Satisfaction & Quality Metrics

User Complaints / Report Rate

Volume of issues reported for:

  • Wrong time zones
  • Missed notifications
  • Ring not triggered
  • Receiver confusion

NPS for Schedule Call Feature

Brief in-app survey asking: "Did Schedule Call make your communication easier?" Provides direct user satisfaction feedback.

Funnel Breakdown

Complete conversation funnel tracking from initial interaction to call completion:

Funnel Stage Metrics to Track
User opens call screen Interaction rate
User clicks "Schedule Call" Feature discovery
User selects date & time Scheduling completion
User agrees to schedule Confirmation rate
Notification is sent Delivery success
User clicks on notification Reminder effectiveness
Call is started Initiation rate
Call is answered Completion success

Business Impact Metrics

While WhatsApp isn't directly monetized, these metrics demonstrate feature value:

Increase in Professional Use Cases

Measure adoption for work meetings, interviews, and remote collaboration through optional tags or ML-based clustering.

Reduction in Missed Calls

Track overall decrease in missed calls across WhatsApp for users who utilize the scheduling feature.

Success Targets

Metric Baseline Target After Improvement
Scheduled Call Completion Rate 40% 70%
Notification Open Rate 55% 80%
Missed Scheduled Call Rate 60% 20%
Repeat Usage Rate 12% 30%
Auto-Ring → Call Conversion N/A 45%

Conclusion

WhatsApp's Schedule Call feature can be evaluated across four key metric pillars:

  • Adoption — How many people try the feature?
  • Engagement — How frequently do they use it?
  • Reliability — Does it perform consistently as expected?
  • Impact — Does it reduce missed calls and enhance communication?

This comprehensive metrics framework serves as a guide to uncover why users miss scheduled calls, where the user journey breaks down, and how new improvements (like ringing at schedule time) boost reliability and user satisfaction.

Market Sizing: Threads Adoption in India

Completed: February 2024
Market Sizing, Guesstimate, User Adoption

Problem Statement

How many users will adopt Threads in India over the next 12 months?

This guesstimate case study aims to measure Threads' potential user growth in India, identify key growth drivers, and define assumptions for a realistic adoption estimate.

Background

  • India represents one of the top 5 social media markets globally
  • Meta has significant existing presence in India through Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp
  • Threads benefits from tight integration with Instagram, enabling seamless onboarding
  • Key competitors include Twitter/X, ShareChat, Koo, Moj, and YouTube Community

Objective

Estimate the number of new users (new signups + reactivated users) Threads could attract in India within the next 12 months, including breakdown of assumptions and validation methods.

Step-by-Step Guesstimate

Step 1: Size the Total Addressable Base

Digital India population breakdown:

  • Total population: ~1.4 billion
  • Internet users: ~900 million
  • Smartphone users: ~800 million
  • Social media users: ~550-600 million

For this estimate, we'll use: Social media addressable base = 560 million users

These represent users who can easily adopt an app like Threads.

Step 2: Define Meta's Reach Among These Users

Instagram serves as the primary entry point for Threads adoption.

Instagram India users ≈ 300-350 million (India is publicly known to be Instagram's second largest market globally)

Using the average: Instagram users in India ≈ 325 million

Not all Instagram users will adopt Threads, but Instagram provides a massive potential user base.

Step 3: Estimate Adoption Funnel for Threads

Threads benefits from several growth advantages:

  • One-tap login via Instagram
  • Auto-follow feature for existing Instagram connections
  • Push notifications through Instagram
  • Influencer migration from other platforms
  • Relevance during major events (sports, politics, entertainment)

Adoption rate assumption: 20-25% of Instagram users will try Threads in the next 12 months

Using the midpoint: 22% adoption rate

Calculation: 325M × 22% = 71.5 million trial users

Step 4: Convert "Trial Users" → "Active Users"

Not all trial users will become active, engaged users. India typically shows high churn rates for new social platforms.

Assumption: 60% of trial users will become monthly active users (MAU)

Calculation: 71.5M × 60% = ~43 million active users

These represent new MAU added during the next 12 months.

Step 5: Add Returning / Reactivated Users

Some existing Threads users who have become inactive may return through notifications or major events.

Assumptions:

  • ~15 million currently inactive Threads users in India
  • 20% could be reactivated through targeted campaigns and feature improvements

Calculation: 15M × 20% = 3 million reactivated users

Step 6: Final Estimate

Component Users
New active users from Instagram funnel 43 million
Reactivated older users 3 million
Total Adoption in Next 12 Months ~46 million

Final Estimate: Threads will gain approximately 45-50 million new adopters in India over the next 12 months.

  • Develop dedicated Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi feeds
  • Create regional trend pages highlighting local content
  • Implement language-specific content recommendations

2. Reduce Onboarding Dropoff

  • Map Instagram interests to personalized Threads feeds
  • Create welcome content packs based on user preferences
  • Implement progressive onboarding to avoid overwhelm

3. Drive High-Intent Traffic

  • Leverage push notifications from Instagram at optimal times
  • Implement cross-content linking from Reels to Threads discussions
  • Create event-based onboarding flows during major occurrences

4. Improve Retention Loops

  • Develop daily discussion topics to encourage regular engagement
  • Launch creator challenges with recognition and rewards
  • Integrate local events and holidays into the content experience

Conclusion

Based on this structured guesstimate approach, Threads has significant growth potential in the Indian market, with an estimated 45-50 million new adopters over the next 12 months. Success will depend on effectively leveraging Instagram's massive user base while addressing localization needs and building compelling content ecosystems that encourage daily engagement.

The key to maximizing this potential lies in reducing onboarding friction, supporting regional languages, creating strong retention loops, and capitalizing on India's event-driven social media engagement patterns.

Product Improvement: Spotify "Support the Artist" Feature

Completed: March 2024
Product Strategy, Feature Design, Creator Economy

The Problem: The Passion-Paycheck Gap

Spotify's discovery engine helps users find great music — but emerging artists struggle financially. With payouts as low as $0.003 per stream, niche artists with loyal fanbases cannot earn sustainable income. This leads to:-

  • Weak creator monetization narrative: Spotify lacks native tools that help emerging artists earn directly from their fans, making the platform less competitive compared to creator-first ecosystems like YouTube or Patreon.
  • Missed opportunity for deeper engagement: Without a built-in support feature, fans cannot deepen their connection with artists inside the app, limiting emotional loyalty, interaction, and long-term engagement.
"I must have streamed this album a hundred times. I wish I could just give them ₹200 directly to say thanks."

Why This Is Important

Platforms that facilitate direct fan-to-artist support are witnessing an increase in creator satisfaction, loyalty, and retention as the creator economy shifts. If Spotify doesn't provide creators with significant revenue streams outside of streaming royalties, it runs the risk of falling behind.

The Opportunity

By allowing fans to support artists without ever leaving the platform, Spotify can improve artist earnings, increase engagement, and solidify its position as a creator-focused platform.

The Solution: A Native "Support the Artist" Button

A seamless, in-app feature that allows fans to directly support emerging artists — without leaving Spotify.

Key Features

1. Seamless Integration

"Support" icon placed where user engagement is highest:

  • Artist Profile
  • Album & Playlist pages
  • Now Playing screen
  • Discover Weekly / Release Radar
  • Spotify Wrapped

2. Flexible Support Models

  • One-Time Support: Any custom amount you want to pay to support the artist.
  • Monthly Subscriptions:
    • ₹150 — Basic supporter
    • ₹300 — VIP tier with exclusive content

3. Artist-First Payout System

  • Dedicated Artist Wallet
  • Withdraw via UPI, PayPal, or bank transfer
  • Stream revenue vs supporter revenue comparison

4. Fan Recognition & Rewards

  • Optional supporter badges
  • Thank-you notes from artists
  • Priority messaging
  • Early access to concerts & merchandise

User Journey

  1. User discovers an emerging artist
  2. Notices the "Support the Artist" icon
  3. Selects one-time or monthly support
  4. Completes payment with UPI, card, or Paypal
  5. Artist receives funds instantly
  6. Fan receives badge + thank-you note
  7. Stronger emotional connection → repeat support

Key Design Decisions

  • Contextual button placement → Added where intent is naturally high.
  • Preset + custom amounts → Simplifies decisions while allowing superfans to contribute more.
  • Tiered membership system → Increases recurring earnings and strengthens fan identity.
  • Artist Wallet → Builds transparency and trust.

Success Metrics

To measure the effectiveness and impact of the "Support the Artist" feature, I defined success across five key dimensions:

Category Key Metrics
Adoption % of artists enabling support, Support button click-through rate, % of listeners viewing support options
Engagement Number of one-time contributions, Repeat supporter rate, Average contribution amount, Time spent on artist profiles after supporting
Artist Impact Monthly earnings from fans, Increase in release frequency, Artist retention rate, Profile visit increases
User Metrics Supporter satisfaction scores, User retention among niche artist listeners
Business Metrics Total support volume, Potential platform revenue share, Increase in listening time for supported artists

Risk Mitigation

Risk Mitigation Strategy
Fraudulent artists KYC + minimum listeners
Low user adoption Perks, badges, early access rewards
Payment friction UPI + Apple/Google Pay + one-tap repeat
Artist spamming Message limits + fan preferences
Privacy concerns Optional/private badges

Final Impact & Strategic Alignment

  • For Artists: Reliable income stream beyond streaming royalties.
  • For Fans: A meaningful, frictionless way to support the artists they love.
  • For Spotify: Higher loyalty, creator-focused positioning, reduced leakage to Patreon, and a more sustainable ecosystem.
